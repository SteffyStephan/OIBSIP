About Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Despite coming from a humble background, he pursued education with determination and became one of India's most respected aerospace scientists.

He joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969 and played a leading role in India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit.

His contribution to India's missile programme earned him the title "Missile Man of India." He later became the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and won millions of hearts through his humility, simplicity and dedication to young minds.

Dr. Kalam spent the final years of his life travelling across the country, inspiring students to dream big, work hard and contribute towards a developed India.

A Legacy That Lives Forever

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam dedicated his entire life to science, education and nation-building. His vision for a developed India continues to inspire millions of students, teachers, scientists and dreamers across the world.

Even today, his speeches, books and ideas motivate young minds to believe that no dream is too big if it is supported by knowledge, discipline and perseverance.
